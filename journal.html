November 2018

New Orleans, USA

In 1915, healer and rumoured voodoo priestess Julia Brown prophesies that she will take her entire town of Frenier with her when she dies. No one listens.

Years later, upon Julia's passing, everyone in town attends her funeral. The same day, the New Orleans Hurricane of 1915 strikes. The Category 4 storm sweeps through Louisiana, flattening small towns and killing all of the Frenier townsfolk.

Locals believe Julia still haunts their swampland graveyard in LaPlace, Louisiana.

In Saulxures we ate tartiflettes and drank Vin d'Alsace and met newborn calves and stargazed from the mountains. I also stargazed at posters of Steven's uncle, winner of the 16th stage of the 1997 Tour de France. (Everyone in the family has a beautiful pair of legs.)

The first time I visited Paris, I hated it. But returning years later with a French man made all the difference. Now we reminisce about wandering up to the Sacré-Cœur with a bottle of wine before taking the night train to his beautiful mountain hometown in the northeast.
